What is Green Productivity (GP)?

Green Productivity is a strategy for enhancing productivity and environmental performance for overall socio-economic development. It involves applying appropriate techniques, technologies, and management systems to produce environmentally compatible goods and services. GP can be applied in manufacturing, service, agriculture, and communities.

Key Principles & Focus

  • Triple Focus: GP integrates Environment (Sustainable Development), Profitability (Factor Inputs), and Quality (Voice of Customer).
  • Guiding Principles: Emphasizes Accountability, Polluter Pays, Precautionary Approach, People-Building, and Competitive Advantage.

The Six Steps of GP Methodology

This systematic framework ensures continuous improvement and problem-solving in Green Productivity initiatives.

Step 1: Getting Started

The initial phase focuses on preparation and foundational understanding.

  • Task 1: Team Formation: Identify and form a Green Productivity Team with diverse expertise.
  • Task 2: Walk Through Survey & Information Collection: Conduct a comprehensive site survey and gather baseline data.

Tools: Flowcharts, Process Flow Diagrams, Eco-mapping, Benchmarking, Material Balance.

Step 2: Planning

Defining the problem and setting clear objectives for improvement.

  • Task 3: Identification of Problems & Causes: Analyze collected information to pinpoint root causes of inefficiencies.
  • Task 4: Setting Objectives & Targets: Establish SMART objectives and performance indicators.

Tools: Brainstorming, Fishbone Diagram, Pareto Diagram.

Step 3: Generation & Evaluation

Developing and selecting the most effective solutions.

  • Task 5: Generation of GP Options: Brainstorm and research potential GP solutions.
  • Task 6: Screening & Prioritization of GP Options: Filter and assess options based on technical, environmental, and financial criteria.

Tools: Cost Benefit Analysis (CBA), Failure Mode Effect Analysis (FMEA).

Step 4: Implementation

Putting the chosen GP options into practice.

  • Task 7: Formulation of Implementation Plan: Develop a detailed plan including resources, timelines, and responsibilities.
  • Task 8: Implementation of Selected Options: Execute the plan, often starting with trials or pilots.
  • Task 9: Awareness Building & Training: Provide necessary training to ensure staff can operate new processes.

Tools: Gantt Chart, Training Needs Analysis.

Step 5: Monitoring and Review

Assessing the effectiveness of implemented options and making adjustments.

  • Task 10: Monitoring & Evaluation of Results: Track performance indicators and analyze results against targets.
  • Task 11: Management Review: Top management reviews the overall GP program's effectiveness.

Tools: Control Charts, Spider Web Diagram, Post-implementation reports.

Step 6: Sustaining Green Productivity

Ensuring long-term integration and continuous improvement of GP practices.

  • Task 12: Incorporate Changes into Management Systems: Formalize successful changes into standard operating procedures (e.g., ISO 14001).
  • Task 13: Identification of New Problem Areas: Proactively identify new opportunities for improvement, cycling back through the methodology.

Tools: Continuous Improvement Cycle (PDCA, Kaizen), Management Systems (ISO 9001, ISO 14001).

Key Tools & Techniques

Eco-mapping

Visual tool to analyze and manage environmental performance, especially for SMEs, identifying problem locations.

Material & Energy Balance

Quantitative assessment of material/energy inputs and outputs to identify inefficiencies and waste.

Cost Benefit Analysis (CBA)

Evaluates financial feasibility of GP options by comparing costs incurred with benefits accrued.

Failure Mode Effect Analysis (FMEA)

Proactive tool to investigate possible causes of failure and incorporate preventive actions.

5S & 7 Wastes

Management techniques focusing on workplace organization, cleanliness, standardization, and eliminating non-value-adding activities (wastes).

Life Cycle Assessment (LCA) & DfE

Evaluating environmental impacts across a product's entire life cycle and incorporating environmental attributes into product design (Design for Environment).
